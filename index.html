About Me

I’m an undergraduate in Environmental Data Science at Virginia Tech, minoring in GIS. My work bridges environmental analytics, spatial modeling, and field ecology to understand how landscapes respond to natural and human disturbances.

Through research and internships spanning wildland fire, hydrology, agriculture, restoration ecology, and disaster recovery, I’ve developed a strong foundation in geospatial and statistical analysis. I use tools like R, Python, and ArcGIS to integrate field data with remote sensing and socioeconomic datasets- helping uncover patterns in water quality, vegetation dynamics, and community resilience.

I’m particularly interested in applying data science to disaster modeling and environmental management, using spatial data to inform recovery strategies and conservation planning. My goal is to combine technical precision with ecological insight to support sustainable decision-making and climate resilience across diverse ecosystems.
